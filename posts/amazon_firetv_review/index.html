Amazon FireTV review

2020-11-19
reviews ,

So I recently bought an Amazon FireTV lite for Rs 2,100 in hopes that I could put my TV to some use. Here’s my experience with it.

The Good

I didn’t realise this at first, but the FireTV is actually some sort of a skin on Android TV I think and this brings in the same familiarity and customisability. Something I long forgotten existed since my recent shift to the Apple ecosystem. It’s worth pointing out that the FireTV posed no performance issues and I faced stutters only occasionally.

The FireTV was extremely simple to set up and start and zero compatibility issues with my TV. I plugged in my FireTV into my old Sony Bravia 21 inches (do they even make these anymore?), attached to power cable to it, (yes it requires external power), entered my Amazon account and it worked out of the box.

Typing the WiFi password was a pain and I had to repeat a similar procedure for Netflix but other apps supported an OTP style login, which was much simpler. Since my TV has built-in speakers with abysmal quality, I had to connect my FireTV to JBL bluetooth speakers. This turned out to be a great setup since I now had my speakers near me and it wasn’t too loud for others in the house.

Now let me make it clear that I subscribe to (or at least share) many services but the fact remains that you simply can not have all movies on these services. This is true especially in India for some reason. So yeah.. long story short, I had to resort to piracy.

How do I watch external videos on FireTV?

FireTV has this other cool feature which I think makes it a sort of Chromecast. I get this “Cast” icon on many apps like Youtube if my phone is connected to the same WiFi as the FireTV. If I had to, I could cast my Youtube video from my phone to TV (but I could just do it natively on FireTV itself, that’s not the point).

I happened to install Popcorn Time on my Oneplus on a whim to see if I could maybe cast it the same way. For those who are not familiar with this app, you can basically search for any reasonably popular movie and it starts streaming instantly using torrents. Casting from this app does work surprisingly well! You just have select a movie, press play, wait for it to buffer and then press the “Cast” icon.

Remember two things though

  • Choose your subtitle on your phone before casting, else you can not select the subtitle on your TV
  • Make sure to change battery optimisation settings for Popcorn time, else Android might kill this app while it is streaming, for freeing memory.

This method works reasonably well but not as well as Netflix or Prime. For one, scrubbing on the phone is completely broken, although scrubbing on the TV works mostly (even though it seems that both are bound bidirectionally) . Even after disabling the memory optimisation setting for Popcorn time, it does get killed for some reason. And you need to restart the app and re-buffer the movie and scrub to previous location, which by the way, it doesn’t remember. But the good part I can decide on any obscure movie on a whim and if all goes well, the movie starts streaming on the TV in 10 seconds. I can also lock my phone’s screen if that wasn’t clear already.

The Bad

I bought the Lite version and I regret it so much! This thing does not even have a damn volume or a power button. Let me assure you that you will be missing it thoroughly and it is definitely not worth the ~Rs500 discount. Just get the normal one!

Alexa search is also not that great, especially for local languages. My grand mother just can not get Alexa to work with Tamil so we sometimes have had to resort to typing on that god awful ABCD on-screen keyboard.

So was it worth it or not?

I’m definitely think it was worth it. I mean 2k and you get loads of functionality? I’m not complaining!
